control
This work is a phenakistoscope developed under the theme of “digital.” It explores the inner trauma of children growing up in today’s digital environment, especially Gen Z, where family control overlaps with surveillance and constant visibility. The looping structure of the phenakistoscope reinforces the feeling of being trapped.
The first layer shows a child walking on top of a house. He keeps moving in circles but never leaves, representing being confined within the family structure.
The second layer is a door that repeatedly opens and closes. The child tries to open it, but a dark shadow shuts it again, symbolizing parental or external control.
The third layer shows a blinking, crying eye, expressing emotional pressure and the sense of being watched in the digital age.
The outermost layer presents a bird restrained by chains. The bird represents the desire for freedom, while the chains suggest restriction.
In another version, I added a spiral made of furniture extending outward from the center, implying that even when trying to leave, the child remains tied to the home environment.
